Politics of the Philippines

The politics of the Philippines is characterized by a multi-party system, with a presidential form of government. The country operates under a democratic framework, with three branches of government: the executive, legislative, and judicial branches.

Executive Branch

The executive branch of the Philippine government is headed by the President, who serves as both the head of state and the head of government. The President is elected through a nationwide vote and serves a single six-year term. The President is responsible for implementing laws, formulating policies, and representing the country in international affairs.

Legislative Branch

The legislative branch of the Philippine government is composed of the Senate and the House of Representatives. The Senate is composed of 24 senators elected nationwide, while the House of Representatives is composed of representatives elected from legislative districts and party-list organizations. The legislative branch is responsible for making laws, approving the national budget, and representing the interests of the people.

Judicial Branch

The judicial branch of the Philippine government is headed by the Supreme Court, which is composed of a Chief Justice and 14 Associate Justices. The Supreme Court is responsible for interpreting the Constitution, reviewing the constitutionality of laws, and resolving disputes between branches of government. The judiciary plays a crucial role in upholding the rule of law and protecting the rights of citizens.
